Which download?

On Windows, there's just one — the installer above. You're done.

On a Mac, the two builds simply match the chip inside, and you only need one. Not sure which? Five seconds: click the Apple menu in the top-left corner, choose About This Mac, and read the line near the top.

Apple Silicon

Get the Apple Silicon version

If "About This Mac" shows a Chip like:

  • Apple M1 / M1 Pro / M1 Max / M1 Ultra
  • Apple M2, M3, or M4 (any variant)
Intel

Get the Intel version

If "About This Mac" shows a Processor like:

  • Intel Core i5 / i7 / i9
  • Anything that starts with "Intel"

When in doubt, pick Apple Silicon. Every Mac sold since late 2020 uses an Apple chip, so unless yours is older, that's almost certainly the one. Grab the wrong one and it simply won't open — no harm done; just come back for the other.

Why this exists.

I listen to WQXR all day. For years that meant keeping the website open in a browser window — scrunched down into a corner, forever getting buried under nine zillion other tabs and windows. When a piece came on that I needed to pause for a phone call, I couldn't find the thing. And that tiny window loved to catch a stray click and wander off to some article.

So WQXR Streamer is the smallest possible fix: the station, packaged as a proper little desktop app that lives in your menu bar (or the Windows system tray). Play and pause are always one click away, right next to your clock. It shows the same now-playing and recently-played information the website does — the host and show, the composer and piece, who's performing — and nothing more. No accounts, no clutter, no second browser window. Just WQXR, where you can always reach it.

What you get.

Everything the website gives you for listening — in a calmer, faster package.

A love letter to WQXR.

WQXR has been New York's classical music station for nearly a century — free, over the air at 105.9 FM, and streaming to anyone, anywhere. It's the hosts who feel like old friends, the live broadcasts from the city's great halls, the late-night programming that keeps you company, and a daily reminder of how much beauty is just a dial away. It is, quite simply, a public treasure.

This app is a fan's thank-you note. It doesn't replace anything WQXR makes — it just gives me a tidier window onto the same wonderful broadcast, and I wanted to share it with other people who love the station as much as I do.

If WQXR is part of your day too, please give back to the people who make it: support WQXR. They earn it every hour. ♥

How to use it. Everything, start to finish.

1 Install it

On macOS:

  1. Download the build that matches your Mac (not sure which?).
  2. Open the downloaded .dmg file.
  3. Drag the WQXR Streamer icon onto the Applications folder in the window that appears.
  4. Open Applications and double-click WQXR Streamer.

On Windows:

  1. Download WQXR-Streamer-Setup.exe and double-click it.
  2. It installs for just you — no admin prompt, no wizard — and launches itself.
First-launch note. Both builds are code-signed — macOS with an Apple Developer ID (and notarized by Apple); Windows via Azure Trusted Signing. If macOS ever hesitates, right-click (or Control-click) the app icon and choose Open once. If Windows SmartScreen shows a blue prompt for this still-new app, click More info → Run anyway — it stops appearing as more people download it.

2 Find it (menu bar / system tray)

WQXR Streamer has no dock or taskbar button. On macOS it lives in the menu bar at the top of your screen; on Windows it's in the system tray (notification area, bottom-right by the clock — click the ^ if it's hidden in the overflow tray). Either way it's the small WQXR wordmark. The first time it launches, the player pops open automatically so you know it's there. While audio is playing, the macOS icon gently pulses.

3 Play and pause

  • Left-click the menu-bar icon to open the player, then press the big round play / pause button.
  • Or right-click the icon and choose Play / Pause — no need to open the player at all.
  • Your keyboard's play/pause media key works too — as does the macOS Control Center Now Playing tile and the Windows media controls (the volume/now-playing flyout and lock screen).

4 See what's playing

Open the player (left-click the icon) and you'll see, top to bottom:

  • The current show and host (e.g. "Middays with Elliott Forrest").
  • The composer, the piece, and the performers on the air right now.
  • View on WQXR — opens the recording's page in your normal web browser.
  • Recently played — the pieces that aired just before this one, with the time each began.

5 Switch stations

Use the small menu at the top-right of the player, or right-click the menu-bar icon, to switch between WQXR 105.9, New Sounds, and Operavore. If you're already listening, it jumps right to the new station; if you're paused, it waits for you to press play.

6 Volume

Drag the slider in the player to set the app's own volume, or click the speaker icon to mute. (This is the app's own volume, separate from your system volume.)

7 Tuck it away

Glance at the player, then click anywhere else and it disappears, leaving just the tray icon. Click the icon again whenever you want it back. Prefer a regular window? Right-click the icon and choose Open Window.

8 Start playing on launch (optional)

By default the app starts quiet and waits for you. If you'd rather it pick up your last station the moment it opens, right-click the icon and turn on Play on launch. Pair it with launching the app at login — macOS: System Settings → General → Login Items; Windows: drop a shortcut in the Startup folder (press Win + R, run shell:startup) — and WQXR is playing the instant you sit down.

9 Quit

Closing the player window just hides it — the app keeps playing in the tray. To quit completely, right-click the icon (menu bar on macOS, system tray on Windows) and choose Quit WQXR Streamer.

10 Update to a new version

When a new version is posted here, download it and quit the running app (step 9), then install over the old one — macOS: drag the new copy into Applications; Windows: run the new Setup.exe. Your station and volume settings are remembered.

If the audio doesn't start right away on a cold connection, give it a second — the app automatically retries and reconnects to the live stream on its own.
